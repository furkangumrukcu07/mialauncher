Mia Launcher — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 14 May 2026

This policy describes how the Mia Launcher Android application (“the App”) handles information. By using the App you agree to this policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App or uninstall it.

1. Data controller

Mia Launcher is an independent distribution. Use the contact method published on your store listing or official channels for questions.

2. Data we process and why

As a home screen launcher, the App typically:

  • Stores preferences locally: Layout, icon packs, theme options, and similar settings are kept on your device for functionality.
  • Uses installed app information: Names, icons, and shortcuts provided by Android may be shown. This is not sold automatically to third parties; it is processed on-device except where you enable features that use the network.
  • Optional network features: If enabled, the App may fetch configuration or announcement content from your official website (for example a JSON file such as live-information.json). Such requests are for informational content and need not send personal identifiers.
  • Optional crash uploads: Only if you explicitly choose to upload from a crash notification may technical log text be sent to a third-party paste-style endpoint (similar to flows inherited from the Lawnchair codebase, e.g. katb.in). This is voluntary.

The App does not embed a known third-party advertising analytics SDK for profiling; this statement applies unless your build dependencies change materially.

3. Third-party services and links

In-app links (support, news, store, social) open external sites governed by their own privacy policies.

4. Children

The App is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. Follow stricter local age rules where applicable.

5. Retention and security

Core data stays on your device. Optional uploads and web requests aside, you do not need an account on our servers. Device security (screen lock, OS updates) helps protect your data.

6. Your rights

Depending on your region (for example GDPR or Turkish KVKK), you may have rights of access, correction, deletion, objection, and complaint. Most launcher data can be managed via system settings or by clearing app data.

7. Changes

We may update this page when features or legal requirements change. For material changes, also notify users via your store listing or in-app notice as appropriate.

8. Open source attribution — Lawnchair and Android (AOSP)

Mia Launcher is a customized distribution derived from the open-source Lawnchair project. Lawnchair is in turn based on Launcher3 from Google’s Android Open Source Project (AOSP).
